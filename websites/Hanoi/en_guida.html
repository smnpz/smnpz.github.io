HANOI RULES

The Tower of Hanoi is a famous mathematical game or puzzle that involves transferring a set of disks from one peg to another, using a third peg as an auxiliary, while adhering to two fundamental rules:

1) You can only move one disk at a time;
2) You cannot place a larger disk on top of a smaller one.

The game was invented by the French mathematician Ã‰douard Lucas in 1883 and is named after the Tower of Hanoi, a legendary structure located in Hanoi, Vietnam.
The Tower of Hanoi problem is a classic example of a recursive problem and can be solved using the recursive "divide and conquer" algorithm.

The optimal solution to solve the Tower of Hanoi with n disks requires 2^n - 1 moves. This means that the minimum number of moves required to solve the game doubles each time an additional disk is added.

The solution can be found recursively by breaking down the problem into smaller subproblems and solving them one by one.
The Tower of Hanoi has been used as an example in many contexts, including teaching mathematical concepts such as mathematical induction and recursive algorithms.
It is also a source of interest for game and puzzle enthusiasts.
